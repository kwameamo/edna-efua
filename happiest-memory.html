The Happiest Memory

Fri, 04 Oct 2024


A trip around the beach, the sun, the sky, and around you.


A palpable excitement as we packed up and hit the road.
We didn’t realize we were making memories; we just knew we were having fun.
The screams as the salty breeze and crashing waves greeted us upon arrival elevated our spirits.
I just knew that was enough experience and memory to last a lifetime.


I found myself at the shoreline with the purity of the sand stretching eternally before me.
You held my hand and sped off with me along the shore, and I couldn’t hold back the giggles as the sand staggered our movements.


Guess what I put in a frame sitting on the wall directly opposite my bed?
“Jia Li.”
You never ceased to remind me how beautiful and good I was and wanted to put it in a slogan that could stick forever, and truly, it has stuck on a wall now.


Playing in the sand, building sandcastles, each time more artistic than the last.
Tussling to see who could put up the tallest tower, ploughing moats, and embellishing our castles with seashells and seaweed.


These moments seemed small, but looking back today, they are treasures buried in the deepest ore of my heart.


We spent hours splashing around the soothing ocean, playing tag and frisbee.
I still have a vivid picture of the guffawing moments as we jumped over waves and the occasional tumble into the surf.
Feeling ostensibly carefree, with the warmth of the sun on my skin and your chortling laughter echoing.


Coincidentally, this day felt like a summer day in Svalbard with the sun never setting above the Arctic Circle, even as the day seemed to wind down.


As the sun began to set, painting the sky in shades of pink and orange, we gathered wood, set up a bonfire, roasted marshmallows, and made s’mores, the gooey sweetness finely complementing the warmth of the fire.


This day reminds me of what Banana Yoshimoto said: “Truly, happy memories always live on, shining. Over time, one by one, they come back to life.”


We spotted a leisure bench, I sat on your lap, shared stories, and reminisced about past vacations, which created a nostalgic atmosphere that made the moment even more unforgettable.


Worn out as we were, we could only eat and drink to the pleasure of our souls.
In the midst of tiredness and endless fulfillment, we decided to settle and relax.
I laid my head on your chest and could only smell your sweet musky fragrance.
I smelled you for hours and couldn’t let go.


After hours of seemingly eternal thrill, we decided to go home.
You dropped me off at home and drove off.
Never heard from you again, only to find out you never got home!


I can only smile because it happened, not cry because it’s over.
This frame rekindles the memories of a lifetime that unfolded, and I can only be grateful for the happy memories.


Thank you for the happiest memory of my life.
